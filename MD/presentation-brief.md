## SECTION 5 — JOB CARDS (Slides 13–17)

*Relevant roles: Admin, CRM Officer, Technical Director*

---

### Slide 13 — What is a Job Card?

**Heading:** Job Cards — workshop intake records

**Content:**
- A Job Card is opened every time a vehicle is received in the workshop
- It records:
  - Which vehicle came in (VIN / plate number)
  - Owner name and phone
  - Fuel level and mileage at reception
  - Type of service requested
  - Accessories present (jack, spare tyre, fire extinguisher, etc.)
  - Assigned technician
  - Notes
- Each job card gets an automatic reference number: **OR-YYYY-NNNNN** (e.g. OR-2600001)

**Visual:** Screenshot of a completed job card detail view (print view is cleaner)

---

### Slide 14 — Opening a New Job Card

**Heading:** How to open a job card

**Steps:**
1. Go to **Job Cards** in the sidebar
2. Click **New Job Card** (top right)
3. Search for the vehicle by plate number or VIN
4. Customer details load automatically
5. Enter: service type, fuel level, current mileage
6. Check off accessories that are present in the vehicle
7. Assign a technician
8. Add any notes or special instructions
9. Click **Create**
10. The job card opens with its auto-generated number

**Visual:** Screenshot of the New Job Card dialog

---

### Slide 15 — Job Card List & Filters

**Heading:** Finding and managing job cards

**Content:**
- The job card list shows all cards with: number, vehicle, customer, service type, status (Open / Closed), mileage, technician, date
- Filter by: Status (Open/Closed), Service Type, Date range
- Search by vehicle plate, VIN, or customer name
- Click any row to open the full detail page

**Visual:** Screenshot of the job card list with filter bar

---

### Slide 16 — Converting to Delivery Note

**Heading:** Closing a job card — generating the delivery note

**Steps:**
1. Open the job card
2. When the work is complete, click **Convert to Delivery Note**
3. Confirm the action
4. The system automatically:
   - Creates a delivery note number (DN-YYYY-NNNNN)
   - Creates a service record for the vehicle
   - Updates the vehicle's last service date
   - Closes the job card
5. A success message appears with the delivery note number

**Note:** This action cannot be undone. Only do this when the vehicle is ready to be handed back to the customer.

**Visual:** Screenshot showing the Convert button and the confirmation step

---

### Slide 17 — Printing & Sharing Job Cards

**Heading:** Print and email job cards

**Content:**
- **Print:** Click the Print button on any open job card. A clean print view opens with company letterhead, vehicle details, accessories checklist, and signature line.
- **Email:** Click the Share button, enter the customer's email address, add an optional message, and click Send. The customer receives a professional HTML email.
- The print / delivery note view includes: company name, TIN number, and job card number automatically.

**Visual:** Screenshot of the print preview + the share dialog

## SECTION 6 — FOLLOW-UPS (Slides 18–21)

*Relevant roles: Admin, CRM Officer, CRE*

---

### Slide 18 — What are Follow-ups?

**Heading:** Follow-ups — proactive customer outreach

**Content:**
- Follow-ups are tasks created for the CRM/CRE team to call customers
- The system creates them automatically based on vehicle service status:
  - **Service Due Reminder** — vehicle is Overdue for service
  - **Service Due in 15 Days** — customer needs a reminder to book their appointment
  - **Lost Recovery** — customer has not been back in 12+ months
- The nightly system job creates new follow-ups automatically every night
- You can also click **Sync Follow-ups** to generate them immediately

**Visual:** Screenshot of the follow-ups list showing different reason badges

---

### Slide 19 — The Follow-up List

**Heading:** Your follow-up queue

**Content:**
- Go to **Follow-ups** in the sidebar
- See all active follow-ups with:
  - Vehicle plate and brand
  - Customer name and phone
  - Reason (Service Due, Lost Recovery, etc.)
  - Status (Pending, Contacted, Appointment Booked, etc.)
  - Priority (Low / Medium / High / Critical)
  - Due date
- Filter by reason or status
- Search by plate number, customer name, or phone
- **Sync Follow-ups button** (top right) — manually generates new follow-ups now

**Visual:** Screenshot of the follow-ups list

---

### Slide 20 — Opening a Follow-up & Logging a Call

**Heading:** How to handle a follow-up

**Steps:**
1. Click on a follow-up to open its detail page
2. See full vehicle and customer information
3. Call the customer
4. Click **Log Interaction** and record:
   - Outcome (Contacted, No Answer, Appointment Booked, Declined, etc.)
   - Notes about the conversation
   - Next contact date (if needed)
5. Save the interaction
6. The follow-up status updates automatically based on the outcome

**Visual:** Screenshot of the follow-up detail page with the Log Interaction button

---

### Slide 21 — Closing a Follow-up

**Heading:** When to close a follow-up

**Content:**
- Close a follow-up when:
  - The customer has booked an appointment ✅
  - The customer has already serviced their vehicle elsewhere
  - The customer has declined and no further action is needed
- Click **Close Follow-up**, add a final note, and save
- Closed follow-ups are removed from your active queue

**Note:** If the customer returns for service (their vehicle gets a job card and delivery note), the system automatically updates their retention status to "Recovered."

**Visual:** Screenshot of the close follow-up dialog

## SECTION 9 — ADMIN FUNCTIONS (Slides 28–31)

*Relevant role: Admin only*

---

### Slide 28 — Managing Users

**Heading:** Adding and managing team members

**Steps to create a new user:**
1. Go to **Settings → Users**
2. Click **New User**
3. Enter: Full name, email, password, role
4. Optionally assign a Permission Group for custom access
5. Click **Create**

**Steps to reset a password:**
1. Find the user in the list
2. Click **Reset Password**
3. Enter the new password
4. User can log in with the new password immediately

**Steps to deactivate a user:**
1. Find the user
2. Click **Edit**
3. Toggle **Active** to off
4. Save — the user can no longer log in

**Visual:** Screenshot of the Users settings page and the Create User dialog

---

### Slide 29 — Permission Groups

**Heading:** Custom permission groups

**Content:**
- By default, each role (CRMOfficer, TechnicalDirector, CRE) has standard permissions
- You can create **Permission Groups** to customise what a specific team or individual can see and do
- Example: Create a "Senior CRE" group that can also see the Retention analytics page
- Assign a Permission Group to any user under Settings → Users

**Steps:**
1. Go to **Settings → Permission Groups**
2. Click **New Group**
3. Name the group (e.g. "Senior CRE")
4. Check/uncheck the permissions you want
5. Click Save
6. Go to Users, edit the user, and assign the group

**Visual:** Screenshot of the Permission Groups page showing the checkbox list of permissions

  - What to show on delivery note printouts

## SECTION 11 — IMPORT CENTER (Slide 34)

*Relevant roles: Admin, CRM Officer*

---

### Slide 34 — Bulk Import

**Heading:** Importing data in bulk

**Content:**
- Go to **Import Center** in the sidebar
- Supported import types: Vehicles, Customers, Service Records, Job Cards
- **How it works:**
  1. Download the CSV template (ask your Admin for the format)
  2. Fill in your data following the column headers
  3. Upload the file in Import Center
  4. The system **validates** first — shows you any errors before importing
  5. Review the preview — fix errors in your CSV if needed
  6. Click **Process Import** to commit the data
- Duplicates are detected and skipped automatically

**Note:** Import timeout is 5 minutes for large files. For very large datasets, split into batches of 1,000 rows.

### Slide 36 — Key Tips & Reminders

**Heading:** Tips for getting the most out of the DMS

**Content (bullet points):**
- Always log every call in Follow-ups — even if it was a no-answer. This builds your contact history.
- The retention status updates automatically every night. Check it in the morning.
- Use the **Sync Follow-ups** button if you need fresh follow-ups immediately (don't wait for midnight).
- When a vehicle is received, always open a Job Card — this is what keeps the service history accurate.
- When work is done, always convert to Delivery Note — this updates the vehicle's last service date.
- Never share your login credentials. Each person should have their own account.
- For any access issues, contact your Admin at **admin@rwandamotor.com**.
- Access the system at **app.rwandamotor.com** from any browser.
